Your Salary Isn't the Problem

By Fikayo Bolarinwa, Founder · Pearlcore · June 2026

You already know how the month goes.

Salary drops. For about two days, you feel like a person who has their life together. Then rent remembers it exists, feeding. Even transport every single morning, unbothered, non-negotiable. Someone needs something. Something breaks. Suddenly, it's the 15th and you're doing math you already know the answer to, wondering how money that felt like something became nothing this fast.

And the worst part isn't even the money running out. It's that you can't explain it. You didn't blow it on anything dramatic. You didn't go on a trip or make a big foolish purchase. You just lived. And living, in Nigeria, on a salary that was already tight before the month started, costs everything you have.

So you tell yourself next month will be different. Next month you'll be more careful. Next month you'll finally open that Piggyvest account and lock something away.

Except next month has the exact same bills. But you do open the Piggyvest. And you do lock something. Then the month hits differently, and you unlock it because the penalty is small enough to pay, and the need is real enough to justify it. Or you start an ajo with some people and it's great until it's your turn to collect and the money goes straight to something urgent and somehow you're back to nothing. Or you just keep it in your account and tell yourself you won't touch it, but you do, with every intention of replacing it, and you never quite get to.

This is not a discipline problem. This is a structure problem.

What's Actually Missing

Every savings method that fails does so for the same reason: the wall isn't high enough.

Willpower isn't a wall. Good intentions aren't a wall. A savings account you can transfer out of at 2am is not a wall. Even a safelock you can break for a penalty isn't a wall, it's a speed bump, and when things get hard enough, speed bumps don't slow anyone down.

What actually works is something that makes the money genuinely difficult to reach. Not impossible. Just difficult enough that "I'll just take a little" becomes a real decision with real friction.

And that's what I'm building with Pearlcore.

The Community

It's free. And simple. Every month, you keep something, whatever your month allows, no fixed amount, no judgment. It goes into a dedicated account you can see but can't touch until December.

That's the deal.

Not "try not to touch it." Not "only for real emergencies." The community agreement is that whatever goes in stays in until the year closes. There are monthly check-ins. There are real people in that Telegram group doing the exact same thing, on the same timeline. The wall is the agreement, and the agreement is harder to break when you're not the only one keeping it.

Will some months still be hard? Yes. Some months life will take everything. This community isn't magic and I'm not going to tell you it stops that from happening. What it does is make sure that whatever you managed to keep before that month hit, survives it. Because you can't access it. Because the wall held.

And then December comes.

December

You've never seen your money just sit there for a whole year. Most of us haven't, because something always gets to it first. But when you get to December and see the full amount staring back at you, months of keeping, untouched, intact, it does something to you. It's not just the money. It's the proof that you can. That you did. Despite everything this year threw at you.

That's what I want you to experience. Even once.

Because once you see what a year of keeping looks like, you don't go back to not knowing where your money went.

Come in. It's free.

If you've tried the apps, tried the ajo, tried starting over every January, try this one too. A community that holds the wall with you. The only thing it costs you is not touching your money until year end.
